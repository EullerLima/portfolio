Path

Since my childhood I've always been very connected with the world of technology, frequenting blogs, playing games online, watching videos and researching ways to improve the functioning of the old computer in the living room.

After a period of indecision about which course to take, I found myself fully in the Systems Analysis and Development course, which showed me an area totally in tune with my profile and career objective: programming.

Despite this, I still took a little risk studying Information Security, where I came across several very important issues for any technology professional, going through topics that involve risks and care in the administration of networks, servers, systems and applications.

During all my journey, I met some back-end languages ​​like C/C++, Java, Python (which I also liked a lot) and Ruby, but it was on the front-end that I found what really shined my eyes, bringing the possibility of creating applications, dynamic pages, content accessible to the general public. The practical semantics of HTML, the infinite possibilities for styling with CSS and the dynamism and interoperability provided by JavaScript made me embrace this way of coding and keep evolving more and more along this addictive path.

There is still a technology that I am very interested in studying, Blockchain. A field that is already a reality and has everything to become more and more present in this connected world we live in.
